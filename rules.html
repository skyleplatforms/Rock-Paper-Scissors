RULES

To play rock paper scissors, two players simultaneously show one of three hand shapes: rock, paper, or scissors.
The winner is determined by the following rules:
Rock beats scissors: A rock can blunt scissors
Scissors beats paper: Scissors can cut paper
Paper beats rock: Paper can cover rock
Tie: If both players show the same shape, the round is a tie and is repeated


In summary, the game is rock-paper-scissors plus a fourth move called "well".
Well is essentially a strong rock: it defeats scissors and loses to paper, but also defeats rock.
The "solution" is to recognize that since rock is never better than well, you should just never play rock.


Once again, Rock wins against scissors; paper wins against rock; and scissors wins against paper.
If both players throw the same hand signal, it is considered a tie, and play resumes until there is a clear winner.


Happy playing!!
